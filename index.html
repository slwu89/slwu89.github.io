Hello, World!

  • I am a second year PhD student in epidemiology with a designated emphasis in computational & genomic biology at UC Berkeley; my advisor is John Marshall, PhD. To date we have worked together on mathematical models for Plasmodium falciparum (malaria) transmission at the Malaria Elimination Initiative (UCSF), probabilistic models of human movement in East Africa, and research into genetic control strategies as part of the DARPA Safe Genes project.
  • I also collaborate frequently with Héctor Sánchez, PhD, on MASH (Modular Analysis & Simulation for Human Health), an agent-based model to investigate transmission of mosquito-borne pathogens, a project lead by David Smith, PhD. We have also collaborated on VCOM (Vector Control Optimziation Model), a differential equation based model for inference of vector control interventions on P. falciparum transmission.
  • I have also previously done research in Sichuan, China investigating genetic biomarkers of susceptibility to infection by Schistosomiasis japonicum, a water borne liver fluke, led by Robert Spear, PhD.
  • My research interests include simulation of stochastic processes, emergent pathogen behavior in epidemiological systems, Bayesian inference for stochastic models, viral phylogenetics, and gene drive.
