Sean Wu: PhD Student in Epidemiology, MPH in Epidemiology & Biostatistics at UC Berkeley

Hello, World! 1

  • I am a second year PhD student in epidemiology with a designated emphasis in computational & genomic biology at UC Berkeley; my advisor is John Marshall, PhD. We work together on mathematical models of infectious diseases and on CRISPR-Cas9 based genetic control for the DARPA Safe Genes project and for agriculture with the Innovative Genomics Institute.
  • I also collaborate with Héctor Sánchez, PhD, on MASH (Modular Analysis & Simulation for Human Health), an agent-based model of mosquito-borne pathogen transmission, led by David Smith, PhD. We also worked on VCOM (Vector Control Optimziation Model), a mathematical model for Plasmodium falciparum transmission.
  • I have also worked in Sichuan, China on genetic biomarkers of susceptibility to infection by Schistosomiasis japonicum with Robert Spear, PhD.
  • My research interests include simulation of stochastic processes, emergent pathogen behavior in epidemiological systems, Bayesian inference for stochastic models, viral phylogenetics, and gene drive.
  • I like computers, movies so bad they become good (movies have toroidal boundary conditions?), programming, food, fresh air, and archery.

My (computer) languages are: R (native), C++ (fluent), Mathematica (conversational), Julia (beginner), and Python (beginner).
My (human) languages are: English (native), Mandarin Chinese (fluent).
