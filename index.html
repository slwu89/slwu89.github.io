Interested in agent-based models (ABMs), complexity & networks science, Bayesian inference for stochastic models, viral phylogenetics, gene drive technology, and data visualization for infectious disease epidemiology.

Hello, World!

I am a first year PhD student in epidemiology with a designated emphasis in computational & genomic biology at UC Berkeley; my advisor is John Marshall, PhD. To date we have worked together on deterministic ODE models for Plasmodium falciparum (malaria) transmission at the Malaria Elimination Initiative (UCSF), and human movement models fit with MCMC and parameterized with survey data from Burkina Faso, Tanzania, Swaziland, and Mali. I have also worked before in Sichuan, China investigating genetic biomarkers of susceptibility to infection by Schistosomiasis japonicum, a water borne liver fluke.

My (computer) languages are: R (native), C++ (fluent), and Mathematica (conversational).
My (human) languages are: English (native), Mandarin Chinese (native).
