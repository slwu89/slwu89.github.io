Sean Wu: PhD Student in Epidemiology, MPH in Epidemiology & Biostatistics at UC Berkeley

Hello, World!

  • I am a third year PhD student in epidemiology with a designated emphasis in computational & genomic biology at UC Berkeley; my advisor is John Marshall, PhD. We work together on mathematical models of infectious diseases and on CRISPR-Cas9 based genetic control for the DARPA Safe Genes project and for agriculture with the Innovative Genomics Institute.
  • I also collaborate with Héctor Sánchez, PhD, on MASH (Modular Analysis & Simulation for Human Health), a suite of agent-based models with mathematical representations of mosquito-borne pathogen transmission, led by David Smith, PhD. We also worked on VCOM (Vector Control Optimziation Model), a mathematical model for Plasmodium falciparum transmission.
  • I spent a summer at the Santa Fe Institute's Complex Systems Summer School 2017 where I collaborated on a project to use commuter transit data for identifying functionally relevant geographic clusters in Singapore.
  • My research interests include stochastic processes, software architecture for large-scale simulation models, applications of complex systems theory and ideas from statistical mechanics to vector-borne disease transmission.
  • I like cooking, music, reading, archery, movies, photography, programming, and mathematics.

My (computer) languages are: R (native), C++ (fluent), C (conversational), Julia (beginner), and Python (beginner).
My (human) languages are: English (native), Mandarin Chinese (fluent).
