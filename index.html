Sean Wu: PhD Student in Epidemiology, MPH in Epidemiology & Biostatistics at UC Berkeley

Interested in agent-based models (ABMs), complexity & networks science, Bayesian inference for stochastic models, viral phylogenetics, gene drive technology, and data visualization for infectious disease epidemiology.

Hello, World!

  • I am a first year PhD student in epidemiology with a designated emphasis in computational & genomic biology at UC Berkeley; my advisor is John Marshall, PhD. To date we have worked together on deterministic ODE models for Plasmodium falciparum (malaria) transmission at the Malaria Elimination Initiative (UCSF), and human movement models fit with MCMC and parameterized with survey data from Burkina Faso, Tanzania, Swaziland, and Mali.
  • I also collaborate frequently with Héctor Sánchez, a PhD candidate in computer science on MASH (Modular Analysis & Simulation for Human Health), an agent-based model to investigate transmission of mosquito-borne pathogens, and VCOM (Vector Control Optimziation Model), a differential equation based model for population level inference of P. falciparum transmission.
  • I have also previously done research in Sichuan, China investigating genetic biomarkers of susceptibility to infection by Schistosomiasis japonicum, a water borne liver fluke. This research is ongoing and is lead by Robert Spear, PhD, and Bo Yang & Yang Liu at Sichuan Province CDC.

My (computer) languages are: R (native), C++ (fluent), Mathematica (conversational), Julia (beginner), and Python (beginner).
My (human) languages are: English (native), Mandarin Chinese (fluent).
